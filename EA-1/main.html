EA-1 Veronica

The Resistivity Temperature \(\rho-T\) Measurement Automation

To increase the experiment processes, I designed and assembled the resistivity-temperature 𝝆-T measurement automation. "EA-1" stands for "Experimental Apparatus No.1", and the nickname of the project "Veronica" means "she who brings victory".

Specifications:
∙Measures 15 oxide or alloy pallets simultaneously
∙Range of Temperature: 70K to 400K
∙Range of Resistivity: \(1\mu\Omega cm\) to \(1G\Omega cm\)

▲The Derivation of the 4-Point Probe Method. \(R_c\) represents the contact resistance (\( \sim 10\Omega \)), and \(R_V\) is the internal resistance of the voltmeter (\( > 10M\Omega \)), such that \(R_V >> R_c\). For small resistance and superconductivity measurements, the contact resistance \(R_c\) becomes significant, making the traditional 2-point probe method inaccurate. The 4-point probe method eliminates the contribution of the contact resistance \(R_c\). (Click to enlarge)

LabVIEW Design

LabVIEW codes control and collect information from the instruments through GPIB. Instruments include Keithley 237 and 617 meters, HP34420A meter, Stanford Research Systems SR830 Lock-in, and Cryo-Con 32B temperature controller.The original LabVIEW code was not modularized, making it difficult to expand. After modularization, the program became scalable to multiple measurement loops.
